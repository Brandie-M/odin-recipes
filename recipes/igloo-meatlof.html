Igloo Meatloaf (1967)

Source: http://rochellesvintagerecipes.blogspot.com/2008/05/igloo-meat-loaf.html#links

If you're looking to please the kids in your life, look no further! This recipe, from the creatives at Family Circle magazine, is perfect for when you want Wednesday night's meatloaf to really shine. Who could resist jumping right when meatloaf is shaped like an igloo, frosted with mashed potatoes and melted cheese.

Ingredients

For the meatloaf:

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 1/2 cup fine dry bread crumbs
  • 6 tablespoons instant minced onion
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  • 1-1/3 cups evaporated milk

For the potato frosting:

  • 1-1/2 cups water
  • 2/3 cups evaporated milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 envelope (3-1/2 oz.) instant mashed potatoes
  • Cheddar cheese slices

Directions

To prepare meatloaf:

  1. Preheat oven to 325F.
  2. In a bowl lightly mix ground beef, crumbs, onion, salt, pepper and evaporated milk just until blended.
  3. Pack firmly into a 1-1/2-quart bowl; turn out of bowl onto a shallow baking pan.
  4. Bake in a preheated 325 degree oven 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 hour 30 minutes.

To prepare potato frosting:

  1. In a 1 -1/2-quart saucepan combine water, evaporated milk, salt and butter; bring just to boiling.
  2. Remove from heat; with whip or fork stir in potatoes; whip briskly.
  3. Frost loaf with potato mixture.
  4. Bake and additional 15 minutes.
  5. Place cheese slices on top of Igloo, return to oven just until cheese starts to melt.

Serves 6-8.
